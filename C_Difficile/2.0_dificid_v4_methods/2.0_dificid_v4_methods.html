Methods

A Markov model

  • Simulated a cohort of patients presenting with an initial CDI diagnosis
  • Estimated the costs, effectiveness, and cost-effectiveness of 48 CDI treatment
    regimens recommended in the 2018 IDSA/SHEA guidelines
Model structure

NOTE: At any point along the journey, patients could die from non-CDI causes.

Assumptions

  • Patients with 2 or more recurrences who failed treatment were assumed not to have
    died from CDI-related causes but would receive treatment again following IDSA/SHEA
    guidelines for patients with 2 or more recurrences
  • Patients who were initially cured following treatment and did not experience a
    recurrence within 2 months of treatment were assumed to have achieved long-term
    cure and no longer had a risk of CDI recurrence
  • Fulminant CDI patients who fail treatment could die from the CDI; those who survived
    were assumed to undergo a colectomy, with a corresponding probability of surgical
    death. Patients surviving the colectomy procedure were assumed to be cured of the
    CDI and no longer at risk of CDI recurrence

Data sources

  • IDSA guidelines and published literature on treatment outcomes

Outcome measures

  • Discounted quality-adjusted life-years (QALYs)
  • Costs, and incremental cost-effectiveness ratios (ICERs)

    ICERs were calculated by dividing the additional cost by the additional
    QALYs gained compared to the next less expensive strategy

    ICERs were interpreted using a willingness-to-pay threshold of $100,000
    US dollars

    Strategies with an ICER < $100,000 were considered cost-effective

Post-time horizon

  • Patients alive at the end of the 1-year time horizon were assumed to be cured of their
    CDI without risk of future recurrence

    These patients were assumed to have an average remaining life expectancy and
    lifetime healthcare costs

Healthcare payor perspective

  • Cost-effectiveness analysis was conducted from a healthcare payor perspective, with
    costs and health outcomes discounted at 3% per year

CDI treatment strategies recommended in IDSA/SHEA 2018 guidelines

  • Treatment strategies were constructed by selecting one of the IDSA/SHEA-recommended regimens for each CDI type and severity
  • This resulted in 48 different treatment strategies, representing all possible combinations of recommended regimens.

  • Epidemiology of CDI, probability of cure, failure, and recurrence of each regimen were taken from published
    medical literature
  • Medication costs were estimated by applying a 40% discount to the average wholesale price (AWP) published through
    Micromedex/Redbook
  • Costs for FMT were taken from CMS and published studies
  • The daily cost of CDI hospitalization was calculated from a published study evaluating the economic burden of CDI
  • All costs from published literature were converted to 2018 US dollars
Sensitivity analysis
  • Cost of fidaxomicin was varied in a 1-way sensitivity analysis to determine the threshold for which fidaxomicin would be a cost-
    effective choice for non-severe, severe, or recurrent CDI
  • Additional 1-way sensitivity analysis was performed varying the cost of FMT, as costs of FMT are variable depending on source
    of stool and where it is being performed. The threshold at which FMT would no longer be cost-effective for recurrent CDI was
    also explored
  • A probabilistic sensitivity analysis (PSA) was performed varying input parameters of interest within a defined distribution
